Bicycle history

A bicycle, or a bicycle (abbreviation of the word velocipede), is a human-powered land vehicle falling into the category of cycles and composed of two in-line wheels, which give it its name. The driving force is provided by its driver (called a ‚Äúcyclist‚Äù), most often in a seated position, by means of two pedals driving the rear wheel by a roller chain. The front wheel is steerable and ensures balance. Its orientation is controlled by a handlebar. The cyclist often has both hands in contact with the handlebars in order to control the trajectory, the braking as well as the change of gears. The bicycle is one of the main means of transportation in many parts of the world. Its energy efficiency is particularly high. Its practice, cycling, is at the same time a daily use of transport, a popular leisure activity and a sport.

Type of bike sports

Cycle sport is competitive physical activity using bicycles. There are several categories of bicycle racing including road bicycle racing, cyclo-cross, mountain bike racing, track cycling, BMX, and cycle speedway. Non-racing cycling sports include artistic cycling, cycle polo, freestyle BMX and mountain bike trials.
